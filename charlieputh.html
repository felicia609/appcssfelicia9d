CHARLIE PUTH

Biography

Charles Otto Puth Jr or Charlie Puth born December 2, 1991 is an American singer, songwriter, and record producer known for his viral success on YouTube. After signing with eleven-eleven following a performance on The Ellen DeGeneres Show, Puth contributed to songs for artists like Cody Simpson and Livingston Taylor while also creating solo material. His debut single, "Marvin Gaye," featuring Meghan Trainor, was released in 2015 and achieved significant success. This was followed by his collaboration on "See You Again" with Wiz Khalifa for the Furious 7 soundtrack, which topped the US Billboard Hot 100 for 12 weeks. Puth gained further recognition with his single "One Call Away," which reached number 12 on the Billboard Hot 100 and topped charts in several countries.

Puth's debut studio album, Nine Track Mind, released in January 2016, received negative reviews but included successful singles like "One Call Away" and "We Don't Talk Anymore." In 2017, he released "Attention" and "How Long," with "Attention" peaking at number five on the Billboard Hot 100. These tracks were part of his second album, Voicenotes (2018), which garnered positive reviews and peaked at number four on the Billboard 200. In 2019, Puth released several singles, including "I Warned Myself," "Mother," and "Cheating On You," while his third studio album was anticipated for release in 2020.

Awards

  • Grammy Awards: Nominated for Best New Artist and Song of the Year for "See You Again."
  • American Music Awards: Won Favorite Adult Contemporary Artist; received nominations for his songs.
  • Billboard Music Awards: Won Top Hot 100 Song; received nominations for Top Streaming Song.
  • MTV Video Music Awards: Nominated for various categories, including Best Collaboration.
  • Teen Choice Awards: Won several awards for his music and collaborations.
  • iHeartRadio Music Awards: Received nominations and wins for various categories, including Best Collaboration.
